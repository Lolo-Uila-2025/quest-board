Teachable Machine: Haumana or Kumu?

Can you train an AI to tell who’s who? In this quest, you’ll use the webcam and Teachable Machine to create a classifier that can distinguish between:

  • Haumana (You guys!)
  • The Kumu
  • Background/nothing

🎯 Your Goal

Create an AI model that can recognize whether a person in front of the camera is a student, a teacher, or no one at all. You’ll train the AI by showing it examples of each using your webcam.

🔧 Steps to Follow

  1. Go to Teachable Machine.
  2. Choose an Image Project → “Standard Image Model”.
  3. Create 3 classes: Haumana, Kumu, and Background.
  4. Use the webcam to record short clips of each class:
    • Ask your classmates to record 10–20 seconds labeled as Haumana”
    • Steal Uncle Cody and Auntie Jas for "Kumu” examples
    • Record an empty background for “Background”
  5. Click “Train Model” and preview the live detection!

✅ Completion

If your model can reasonably tell if a student or teacher is in front of the camera, and say “background” when no one is there — you earn +10 points!

💡 Extra Ideas

  • Try using different lighting conditions to test robustness.
  • Try making the teacher class stricter: e.g., only when wearing a lanyard or standing.
  • Use it as a fun detector: “Uh oh, teacher detected!”
