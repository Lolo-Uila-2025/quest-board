Quest: Serial Monitor Basics

In this quest, you’ll learn how to communicate with your Arduino using the Serial Monitor. This is how your programs will “talk” to you!

What You’ll Learn

  • How to start serial communication
  • How to print text or numbers to the Serial Monitor

Instructions

  1. Open the Arduino IDE and create a new sketch.
  2. Inside the setup() function, start serial communication with this line: 
    Serial.begin(9600);
    This sets the communication speed. Stick with 9600 for now.
  3. Use Serial.println() to print messages. Example: 
    Serial.println("Hello from Arduino!");
  4. Upload the code to your Arduino.
  5. Click the Serial Monitor button (top-right magnifying glass in the IDE) to see your message appear.
  6. Add at least two more `Serial.println()` lines with different messages or numbers. Get creative!

Once you’ve verified the output appears in the Serial Monitor, show your teacher to claim +5 points.
